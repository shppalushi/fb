Fibrogastroscopy

Description

Instrumental diagnostics of the digestive tract is prescribed by a gastroenterologist to confirm (refute) the alleged diagnosis and choose the most effective therapeutic tactic. The procedure for FGS is prescribed when the following symptoms occur: pain in the upper abdomen, indicating the development of erosive processes of the gastric mucosa or the progression of the inflammatory process heartburn and frequent belching are the conditions often associated with the onset of peptic ulcer loss of appetite or its complete absence persistent nausea swallowing disorder unreasonable weight loss, which may be an indicator of the development of a malignant tumor process Contraindications for gastroscopy in Kharkov are hypertensive crisis, bleeding disorders, a stroke or heart attack, bronchial asthma and a number of other pathological conditions, the course of which should be reported to the attending physician before the appointment of the procedure. This will facilitate the adoption of a reasonable decision and the search for optimal diagnostics tactics.


Examination and diagnosis - English

Esophagus with weakened mucosa and abnormal permeability, gastroesophageal junction at a distance of 40 cm from the dental arch. Has Lax Cardia.
Stomach with regular shape and weakened peristalsis. Relief of normal corpus drops, fundus antrum with hyperthermic mucosa, highly nodulated erythematosus and with erosions. Hyperthermic pylori. Duodenal bulb deformed with deep ulcer and covered with fibrin and hematomas, surrounding mucosa with many erosions.
Insufficiency of the esophageal sphincter, diffuse erythematous gastritis, nodular, active duodenal bulb ulcer.
